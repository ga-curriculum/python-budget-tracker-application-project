## Introduction

At the end of the unit, students will have the opportunity to present their unit projects. This presentation will be a live demo and walkthrough of your application, allowing you to showcase the functionality and features you've built.

### Key Points:

- **Live Demo**: Your presentation should include a live demonstration of your application. Walk the audience through the main features, showing how your application works in real time.

- **Optional Pre-Recorded Demo**: Some students may choose to prerecord a live screen share demonstration of their application’s functionality. This recording can be shared with employers, instructors, or other students.

- **Presentation Length**: Keep your presentation concise—aim for **_no more than 5 minutes_**.

- **Presentation Structure**:
- 1. **Start with the README**: Begin your presentation by introducing your application through the README file in your GitHub repository. Explain what your application does and its key features.
  2. **Live Demonstration**: Follow up with a live demonstration of your application, highlighting its main functionality and user interaction.

This is your chance to show off the hard work you've put into your project, so make sure to prepare and practice your presentation to ensure it runs smoothly.
